Which Bike Should You Choose as a Beginner?

Frederik Beck · 19 June 2025 · 1 min read

When you’re new to cycling, it’s tempting to go straight for the sleek carbon bikes you see in races and ads. But here’s the truth: aluminum is your friend when you’re starting out.

Why Not Carbon?

Carbon frames offer incredible performance, no doubt. But carbon is more brittle and not ideal if you’re still developing your handling skills. A crash or even overtightening a clamp can damage a carbon frame. Modern aluminum frames are far more forgiving and often cheaper.

Frame Size & Setup

Getting the right frame size is crucial. A bike that’s too big or too small will be uncomfortable and inefficient. Most major brands (Canyon, Giant) offer online size calculators.

Buying on a Budget

Where you can safely save money:

  • Rim brakes instead of disc brakes
  • Quick-release wheels instead of thru-axles
  • Used bikes from trusted sellers

Look for a reputable brand (Canyon, Scott, Trek, Specialized, Giant), aluminum frame, Shimano 105 or Ultegra drivetrain, and good condition — no cracks, rust, or worn parts.

Personally, I’d pick a road bike or XC MTB over a gravel bike — but it’s really a lifestyle choice at the end of the day.
